Last Thursday's reading group was one of our best attended yet — eighteen people crammed into the back room, tea flowing, margins full of annotations.

## What We Discussed

Federici's central argument — that the witch hunts were not a medieval relic but a foundational tool of capitalist primitive accumulation — sparked an intense, wide-ranging conversation.

We spent a long time on Chapter 3, "The Great Caliban," discussing how the disciplining of the body was essential to the creation of the modern worker. Several participants drew connections to contemporary workplace surveillance and the gig economy.

## Key Quotes That Stuck

> "The body has been for women in capitalist society what the factory has been for male waged workers: the primary ground of their exploitation and resistance."

This line alone could fuel an entire evening of discussion. And it nearly did.
